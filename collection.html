The arowana is an extremely aggressive fish. Full size arowanas are two to three feet long, but can jump six feet out of the water to catch birds and bugs on branches.

They are highly coveted aquarium fish, selling for hundreds to tens of thousands of dollars, depending on the species.

Found in 400 million year old fossils, the coelacanth was originally thought to have gone extinct long ago. However, living coelacanths were discovered off the coast of South Africa in 1938.

Coelacanths secrete excessive, mucus and oil, making them incredibly slimy. Fishermen avoid them since the mucus and oil makes them taste terrible and cause people who eat them to get sick.

The handfish doesn't usually swim. Instead, it sticks to shallow areas and moves around by walking on the bottom using its large fins.

They live in small groups over very limited areas, not found outside southeast Australia and Tasmania, with one species limited in range to the estuary of a single river. The rarest handfish species have gone up to 20 years without being sighted.

An expert in camouflage, the monkfish sits on the bottom of the seafloor, buried in the sand or hiding in the seaweed. They use the spines on their heads like a lure to attract their prey.

Monkfish is an expensive food, and a frequent target of commercial deep-sea fishing. Unfortunately, methods of catching monkfish involve scraping the seafloor and are damaging to marine environments.

The napoleonfish is a smart predator which can work with other fish to catch prey, and even eat sea urchins by breaking them open against a rock.

They are one of the biggest fish that live in coral reefs. Male napoleonfish can grow to six feet long. However, they are all born female and may change their sex as they get older.

Live sightings of the oarfish are incredibly rare, since it lives deep in the open ocean. Oarfish that make it up towards the surface of the ocean usually die due to the change in pressure. Dead oarfish washing ashore have been mistaken as sea serpents. The first video of a live oarfish was taken in 2001.

They are one of the longest existing fish, capable of growing more than thirty feet long.

The sawfish uses its specialized snout to sense electric currents from other fish moving. This lets them hunt for food in murky water. They also use their snout as a streamlined tool to cut through the water and strike their prey quickly.

Every species of sawfish is endangered due to the use of their fins in shark fin soup, despite sawfish being rays, not sharks.

The sockeye salmon varies in appearance depending on where it is located. They live in the ocean as adults, but migrate to freshwater lakes to spawn. Sockeye in the ocean are blue and silver but are red and green in lakes and rivers. Some populations of sockeye are landlocked and spend their entire lives in lakes and rivers.

Males with larger humps have higher social status.

The sturgeon is a very large, long-lived fish that can grow over twenty feet long and three thousand pounds, depending on the species.

Sturgeon are the source of the most expensive types of caviar. Sturgeon fishing and sturgeon caviar production are banned in the US and Canada because they caused the extinction of some species of sturgeon.
